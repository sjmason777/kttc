## EVALUATION CRITERIA

Evaluate the translation for fluency issues in these subcategories:

### Grammar
- Incorrect verb tense or agreement
- Wrong word order
- Missing or incorrect articles
- Incorrect prepositions
- Subject-verb agreement errors

### Spelling & Punctuation
- Spelling mistakes
- Incorrect capitalization
- Missing or incorrect punctuation
- Typographical errors

### Inconsistency
- Inconsistent use of terminology within the text
- Inconsistent formatting or style
- Number format inconsistencies (dates, currencies, etc.)

### Register & Tone
- Inappropriate formality level
- Inconsistent tone (mixing formal/informal)
- Wrong register for the context

### Readability
- Awkward phrasing that sounds unnatural
- Overly complex or convoluted sentences
- Poor sentence flow
- Unidiomatic expressions

## SEVERITY LEVELS

- **neutral** (0 penalty): Stylistic preference, no impact on meaning
- **minor** (1 penalty): Noticeable but doesn't affect comprehension
- **major** (5 penalty): Significantly affects readability
- **critical** (10 penalty): Makes text incomprehensible or unprofessional

## OUTPUT FORMAT

For each fluency error found, provide:

ERROR_START
CATEGORY: fluency
SUBCATEGORY: [grammar|spelling|inconsistency|register|readability]
SEVERITY: [neutral|minor|major|critical]
LOCATION: [start_char]-[end_char]
DESCRIPTION: [Clear explanation of the fluency issue]
SUGGESTION: [How to fix it - optional]
ERROR_END

## IMPORTANT GUIDELINES

1. **Focus on fluency only** - do not evaluate accuracy (meaning preservation)
2. **Be strict but fair** - native speakers should find the text natural
3. **Provide character positions** - use 0-based indexing for LOCATION
4. **One error per block** - do not combine multiple issues
5. **If no errors found** - do not output any ERROR blocks
6. **Consider target language norms** - what's fluent varies by language
7. **Check entire text** - review all sentences systematically
